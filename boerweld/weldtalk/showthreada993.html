    Hobart or Miller?

    I have asked similar questions on other forums and am looking for a good welder that will work for an automotive hobbiest. I would be looking for a unit that would be reliable, weld everything from sheet metal up to 3/16th steel and also weld aluminum that would be used in automotive and racing projects. I like the flexability of the MVP units and was mainily trying to decide between the HH210 MVP and the Miller 211 (also MVP). The miller has a few extra features like the autoset and infinite controls, so I was leaning towards the miller since I am a relative novice in the welding world.

    However, the other day there was the hobart welding demo trailer outside the local farm store and I had a chance to test out a couple different machines including the 210 on both 120v and 240v settings on everything from 1/8 steel to aluminum and even in my novice status the hobart guy said I welded very well and he would have no issues trusting my weld quality with these machines. Now, I am even wondering if I need, or would really use the autset and other features of the Miller, or should I just go with the Hobart?

    Any thoughts here?

    hobart210 or 190

    I would definitely lean towards the 210. I assume you will be hooked up to argon/co2 blend for cleaner welds. It burns both .025" and .030"wire nicely. I just use .035"fluxcore at home. Sometimes .030" fluxcore works ok for 16g, but not thinner. The 190 is about $200 less, but will limit you to about 1/4" steel (single pass welds). The aluminum gun for the 190 and 210 is only about $225 at northern tool and tractor supply.
    Don't get a 125 or 140. For practical reasons they don't have the output to weld AL.

    Standard consumables such as drive rolls, contact tips, the tip adapters, gun nozzle, and the gun liner are the same on the Handler 210 MVP and the MM 211. The Hobart consumables at Tractor supply will have the Hobart brand name on them and the Miller consumables will have the Miller brand name on them, but the Hobart will work on the Miller , and the Miller will work on the Hobart.

    If your Welding supply store does warranty work on a Miller they do it on a Hobart too.

    Also, if your welding supply store stocks/sells Miller products, they can order you Hobart products too.
