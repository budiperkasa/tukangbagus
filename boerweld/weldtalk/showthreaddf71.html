    New 210mvp mixed feelings...

    New to the forum, and kinda new to welding. My buddy gave me a campbell hausfeld 75a flux welder he didn't use any more, It's an ok machine, I built a rampgate for my utility trailer with it, but I felt like it was time to upgrade, After weeks of reading and research I decided to pull the trigger on a 210mvp. This is my first "real" welder. So far I've only played with some scrap 1/8" angle with .030 flux, and this machine is HOT. Going by the recommended settings it will almost blow through 1/8" Had to back the voltage and speed back a fair amount. Tomorrow I'm going to go buy an 80cf 75/25 bottle. Was trying to put it off as long as I can because they're about $250 filled for the first time around, then about $30 for swaps.

    So far it seems it's going to be a good one, but I haven't used it but about 20 minutes today. My ONLY gripe is that every review I've read about this machine, it comes with a miller gun, mine didn't. (even the 190's at the local atwoods had one in the box) That's one of the reasons I went with this one, among others, but I'm kind of disappointed that it didn't come with one. But granted, I only paid $830 shipped for it, and the miller equivalent was $1200. I can't gripe too much.

    There are multiple taps on the distribution transformer. The power company will typically put a recording voltmeter on your side of the transformer to collect voltage data for some amount of time, then decide what tap to use to set it.

    Going from the lower to higher tolerance for a nominal 115 VAC service can change the output of a MIG welder one or more taps. You will need to dial the welder in for your particular situation, then if the power company makes a change, adjust the welder again if the service voltage changes much.
