    I bet i have everyone beat on this one !!!!!

    I bought a Lincoln 180 last summer for $15.00. I had to spend $100 to put new cables on it but it runs better than a welder that has aluminum in it. The copper wound ones are bullet proof. Mine was made in 1947 and they still make some parts for it.

    If you post the code number on the front of the welder I think I can find out when it was made. Also need to know if it has a cooling fan.

    In 1977 lincoln went from copper wire to aluminum wire and had to install a fan to keep it from melting. The one I have didn't have a fan but I modified it with a fan and now I can weld all day and not have it over heat. It welds much better than all of my more expensive welders.
