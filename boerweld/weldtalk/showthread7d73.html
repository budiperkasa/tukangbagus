    New Lincoln Idealarc Tig 250/250

    Hello All,

    I just got a new to me tig welder for what I consider a very good price. The unit is a Lincoln Tig 250/250 it has AC/CD and High frequency. It came with all of the accessories like a 25' water cooled torch, shielding gas regulator, good ground and pedal control.

    So, here are the problems:

    First, the water cooler is a home made 5 gallon bucket and pump setup. I don't see anything wrong with the idea in general but the execution was not impressive, there were mismatched lines and patches throughout the system. I am planning on replacing the reservoir (bucket) and all of the lines with 3/8" rubber tube with as few connections as possible. Question 1: will air compressor hose handle the heat and pressure in this setup? I also plan on using hose barbs and hose clamps at all connections, I am pretty confident that these will give me leak free connections but if anybody has a differing opinion I would love to hear it.

    Second, Probably because of the ghettoness of the water cooler someone has attempted to weld with no water in the torch causing the power line to melt through the tube destroying it. Question 2: I have a used replacement on the way via eBay but I wanted to see if there are any other problems that I should look out for because of this. I checked the torch head itself and the water return and gas lines that were bundled with the power line and all seem to be in good shape.

    Third, The outlet on the front of the unit which will run the water pump only supplies power while welding, not when the machine is switched on. Anybody see a problem with that? I would personally like it to run continuously while the welder is on. At the very last resort I could find whatever interrupts the power and bypass it because it is on the same circuit as the fan which runs continuously.

    Fourth, the gas solenoid was relocated to outside of the welder and screwed onto the face. from the factory there was a little plate that held the gas connection and the solenoid was inside. I will probably end up fabricating a SS 1/4" NPT nipple to a plate and mounting it in a stock location unless someone has a source for the OEM Parts.

    Thanks in advance, you guys were a great help the last time I had a welder problem so I knew right where to go.

    I had a Lincoln 250/250 for about 15 years before I got my present Syncrowave 200. The Lincoln is a great machine. I almost wish I had kept mine.

    My Lincoln came with a home-built water cooler with a small pump, It has a ten gallon stainless steel tank but I never run more than 4 gallons in it. After a few years, the pump failed and I replaced the pump and motor with a procon pump and matching drive motor. They are on eBay all of the time. I also added a pressure gauge and a visible fluid return. I run that cooler on the Miller most of the time. It is powered on whenever the machine is turned on.

    On my Lincoln the power outlets on the front were powered whenever the machine was powered on. I wouldn't care for a system that only came on when welding is initiated. Torches are more than $200 now so any lag in cooling will ruin your torch.

    The Procon is the pump of choice for liquid cooling. I also have a Bernard cooler that holds 2.5 gallons. It as a Procon pump as well. That one has been running for more than 20 years. I also added a pressure gauge on the Bernard to give a visible check on its operation. I look at the pressure gauge each time before I start welding. Now and then, I will alternate with the Bernard and the home- built unit to keep them both working.

    I sometimes run a couple of gallons of Miller Coolant with the rest distilled water but I have also used Dex-Cool and distilled water for years at a time and I've been using the same torch for nearly 20 years.

    If you had s sink in your shop, you could run tap water. When I was in welding school, that's the way they did it in the school shop.

    Good luck.

    EDIT: I was thinking about your statement that the outlet on the front of the machine only supplying power when you are welding. Are you sure of that? I think that they are supposed to be powered all of the time.

    I recall now that the Lincoln 250/250 TIG Stick unit may have a water solenoid in addition to the gas solenoid. The reason that the water solenoid was used was for a city water connection. A hose is run from a sink tap and the water is left on all of the time. Thus, when you start to weld the solenoid opens and and you start to weld, water will flow. This is fine if the solenoid doesn't fail. If it does, the torch will instantly be ruined.

    When you are running a water cooler, there is simply no need for the water solenoid since the cooler should be running when the machine is switched on.

    You asked about whether air compressor hose will work with "the heat and pressure" of your setup. Well, there is almost no heat if the cooler is running. I've welded for hours at a time and the liquid never even gets warm. The pressure readings that I get are usually 50-60 psi so that would be well below what you would encounter in an air compressor.

    But why not take the time and trouble to go to a welding service and buy the correct tubing. I don't know what your budget is but it's a one-time purchase.

    In any case, you should check the front panel 120 VAC plugs to see if they are on all of the time. My Lincoln manual shows that they are hard wired in and are to be used as accessory plugs for anytime that the machine is switched on.

    Here is a picture of my Bernard 2500 alongside of the home built cooler. I welded the stands for both of them. Notice that the pressure gauge on the home-built unit is reading about 60 psi while it is powered on.
