    I didn't read all the posts in this thread. Forgive me if someone already said this.

    Generally, the larger welders lose capability on the low power end of the scale. The larger welders are better at thicker metal welding.

    If you plan on welding a lot of THIN sheetmatal, look at the welder specs. A HH140 can weld thinner sheetmetal than a HH175, based on the specs/charts. If you're really good, you might get by with a 200-250 amp class machine to weld THIN sheetmetal.

    For THIN sheetmetal, you may be better off with a HH125 class machine, dedicated for that purpose. (At the very least, you need to use .023 wire).

    Disclaimer: I'm not familiar with welding THIN (auto) sheetmetal with the larger welders or their capabilities.

    I never have a problem turning it down. If I was really serious about keeping 023 around might buy a second machine for it. The 210 units really give it a little punch to make it useful and allow for most hobby work, repair jobs for home. small biz etc. Only on rare occasion would it make sense for me to own a better machine.

    If time was money wouldn't blink on a new 250 but the bang for the buck would be 2 machines. A 210 class feeder with a spooly and a Maxstar 150 S or equivalent. If I wasn't in the trade since high school, in business off and on, career welder etc I wouldn't have all this. Small genset if I just had to be portable.

    There is a time and place for tig but the real work is done with these and to tell the truth 90% od what I do stick could be done wire if its what was at hand. Its a matter of scale and duty, I weld something every day, inch or rod or 2 here and there.

    I have bigger machines, its not because I actually use them but a bit do the fact I own them for a long time or got them cheap, can store it and its not beyond me to contract a project on occasion although that is more rare and I got one truck bed repair I will fire up a 250 for up, last thing I can remember a year ago used a # of wire on a weld and 2 years ago about 30# on a project.
