    Hi Mike, that's a caged needle bearing......it runs on a hardened shaft and in a hardened shell/sleeve in the housing.

    The bearing housings have a gymbal type of attachment that centralises the shaft and is an early form of self centralizing bearings, now taken over by the plumber block type with ball races having a semi spherical outer race to allow it to pivot in the housing.

    It's probably getting hot because the grease cannot circulate.....i.e. too much grease.

    This is the same situation as your car's front wheel hubs where you fill with 3/4 the volume with high melting point grease.

    Too much grease and the friction of the grease churning around will cause it to heat up.

    This is typical of ball and roller bearings and doesn't happen with plain bearings that just slide on the grease or oil film.

    Seals must be in good order or you'll get dirt into the bearing and it'll jam up.

    Check the condition of the shaft and the housing bearing surfaces........the rollers look to be in good condition.

    Belt tension will be by ear, (unless you have the hand book) and you just allow about 3/4" up and down play in the middle of the belt, as the motor is on a swing arm the adjuster jacking bolt against the base frame will allow you to pull the motor down to get the tension right.

    Too much tension and you'll wear the bearings in the motor and swing arm frame prematurely.

    BTW, in the last photo of the motor, the belt is too slack and will slip if you run the lathe.

    If the bottom of the motor base is resting on the floor or bench top, IE, no room for further adjustment, they probably fitted a belt that was too long or it stretched, hence no further room to re-tension downwards.

    Anyways, if you still have room under the motor base, the belt adjuster needs the two lock-nuts slacked off and the jacking bolt tightened against the face of the base.
    Ian.

    Greatest likely cause of heat would be the collars set-screwed into notches or flats
    too tight against the journals, rubbing and/or binding.
    Second greatest likely cause of heat would be missalignment on your jack-shaft arrangement.

    Ideally V-belts in good condition, and properly fit to the V's and sized properly to load
    should be able to run with only snug tension.

    Cary'ing it's rated load the belt and the pully should not be too hot
