    Torch cooler from "What-have-you".

    Around 1990, I was very impressed with two fellows from Australia and their radiator mfg opperation.
    ? King Bros. it might have been. They bought fin-rolling machinery my employer (then) made.
    I went thru their scrap-book which showed how much of their machinery was hand-made,
    or modified using "What-have-you" It deepened my passion for doing the same.

    I've built tooling many times using what ever I allready had available, rather than ......
    .... in their words "back home there usually isn't a place on the other side of town
    for anything and everything you might need!" Resoursfullness is next to Godlyness you might say.

    I found an old stand-alone Bernard pump a few years back. I wanted a unit that needed no fan.
    Old Miller welder sleds were a big steel tub with wheels and a pump. No fan, as the tub/reservour
    dissapated the collected heat. I wanted a cooling tower arrangement, and had some cut-offs
    of that awfull modular machine-frame cr@p, that tries to pass for welded custom machine frames.
    It has allot of surface area and convection finishes the job.

    Some tubing from my last failed kitchen sink sprayer for the intake tube.
    An old Jerry-Jug for the tank, I would have preferred a brass or aluminum tank
    to aid in heat dissipation, and may go that rout if I turn one up. Some vinal tubing
    (had to go buy some more) this is not a "purist" effort... mind you.

    And it is small, and will cary easilly once I band the jug to the Bernard.
    Only regret is "why did I drill thru the Bernard sticker" I can't remember......
    The only difference is where the hole on top is?"
    Phil

    The Philler-Mattic Tig Torch cooler!
