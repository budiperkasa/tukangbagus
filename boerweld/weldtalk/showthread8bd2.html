    Your change in arc could have been caused by too light of drive roll pressure, or incorrect V groove for wire diameter being ran. Since the unit should have been shipped with an .030-.035 smooth V/ .030 - .035 knurled V, I doubt you were using an incorrect sized drive roll.

    If you are border liner on have just enough wire speed and the wire starts slipping at the drive roll it's going to change the arc noticeably . Too tight of a loop in the gun lead can cause you issues too.
    MigMaster 250- Smooth arc with a good touch of softness to it. Good weld puddle wetout. Light spatter producer.
    Ironman 230 - Soft arc with a touch of agressiveness to it. Very good weld puddle wet out. Light spatter producer.

    I can say avoid fourney wire, that stuff is not up to snuff.
    In my experience, the W A solid wire that I ve ran produced a clean crisp arc, however, weld puddle wet out was on the sluggish side. Lincoln's L-56 is one of the best in the area of puddle wet out. Hobart's HB-28 use to have a real good wet out to it too. HB-28 has taken a step backward in this area, as well as arc quality, since it has started coming from China, instead of the US or Italy.

    All the Forney solid wire, I've tried on a Hobart Handler 187, 210, 210MVP, or Ironman 230 performed well. My current roll of .023 is Forney, and it produces a soft smooth low end arc on the Ironman 230.
