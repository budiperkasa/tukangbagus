    Welding on a square trailer jack questions

    Let me begin by saying, I am novice welder with a new HH140. I am getting ready to weld a Ramjack square trailer jack to my 16ft utility trailer. I have already made a pocket using angle iron, which will allow me to weld to the sides of the square jack. I believe that the tube is 1/8", and the angle is the same.

    As a novice, I am a little concerned about the possibility of burning through the square tubing as I weld it to the pocket that I have built. Here are my questions:

    Should I take the jack apart, prior to welding?
    Should I just tack and move, instead of a continuous weld?
    Using 30 wire, should I cut the power down some from the chart recommendations?

    Any advice will be appreciated.

    should not be a problem wit a hh140

    You are going to need to turn up the machine all the way to start with. Even if you do burn into the jack you are not going to have enough heat to grab the leg inside the outer tube. If you are really worried about this, turn the jack over and squirt a bunch of WD40 into the jack. If the retractable tube has a lot of oil on it a HH140 will NOT have enough heat to quickly penetrate it.
