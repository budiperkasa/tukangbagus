    Do these voltage #'s sound about right?

    I used my DMM to get the voltage from each tap on my 210mvp, did it on 120 and 240v.

    220v 120v
    unloaded loaded unloaded loaded

    1. 19 15.5
    2. 21.5 17.5
    3. 24.5 19
    4. 26.9 20.5 18.9 13
    5. 29.6 21.5 21.8 14
    6. 32.1 23.5 24.8 17
    7. 34.9 24.5 28.3 20


    The reason the "unloaded" is more precise is because I wasn't having to hide my eyes and read a meter at the same time while striking an arc. And the "loaded" #'s are an average of what I was getting, it was bouncing around a little, these are the #'s that showed up more than once. Some a little higher, some a little lower. Also tagged the wires at the polarity block inside the door, idk if that makes a difference or not. Are these #'s close to what it should be?

    Just for giggles you should verify the input line voltage on occasions. Being a x-former machine the output is a ratio of the input. Not that it is anything to be concerned with, just be aware that those ouputs can vary by slight amounts based on a high input vs a low input.
