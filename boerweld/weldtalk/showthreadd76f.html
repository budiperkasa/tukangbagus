    Originally Posted by gwmotorsports
    Actually you can put a root in with 7018. When I did my A level welding course (3rd and final year for welding schools in Canada) we had to do a series of welds of open root welds in every position on plate using 8018. If I remember correctly we had to switch the polarity to DCEN for the root. Doing the welds on flat and horizontal were easy. Vertical was a bit more challenging and overhead was just plain stupid. I spent a few days trying to get the overhead down and never did come up with a perfect weld. I don't even know why this was included in the course because you will never see this done, but the instructor figured it was just an old procedure and they never took it out of the course for some reason.
    The last pipeline test I took the inspector gave me the welding procedure for 7016 or maybe 7014 open root on a bellhole and branch.     Originally Posted by mcostello
    How you present yourself will influence how, why, and how many replies you will get from some very talented people here.
    I agree, as a newly hired welder, I will add some advice here...not trying to start anything else up, but your personality is at least as important as your skill. You could be the greatest welder in the world, but if you act like an a$$, no one is going to hire you. Learn to take advice from more experienced people, I spent many hours in welding training here, and my welds have already gotten 2x's as good as they were after just a month actually welding on the job. I give a lot of the credit for that to the guys in my crew. This goes for most older guys, you show them respect, pay attention, and don't talk back to them, they will make you into a great welder. Tone 'er down a little and u'll be fine, good luck.
